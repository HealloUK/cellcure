Researches and Clinical Trials

Many research and clinical studies show that stem cells have a theuropatic effect on many diseases including autism, multiple sclerosis, diabetes type 2, stroke, arthritis, COPD, Parkinson's.

Where to obtain?

Stem cells can be obtained from bone marrow, adipose tissue, umbilical cord etc. Patients may use their stem cells or receive them from a donor.

Growing

Stem cells are cultured in the laboratory environment under appropriate conditions.

Administration

Cells can be administered intravenously, intramuscularly, intrathecally, or locally, depending on the disease.

Stem cells given to the body sense the signals coming from the damaged area and migrate to that area.

Stem cells in the damaged area stimulate the surrounding cells to repair the damaged tissue by secreting substances like cytokines, chemokines and growth factors.
