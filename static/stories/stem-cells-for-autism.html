What is Autism?

Autism spectrum disorders (ASD) are a range of neurodevelopmental conditions that causes impairment of the brain regions responsible for speech, attention, memory, concentration and emotions.

Lifelong condition

It causes lifelong social disorders, communication difficulties, and repetitive behavior patterns.

Autoimmune disorder

Many scientific studies have shown that autism is associated with an autoimmune disorder.

Inflammation

High concentrations of chemicals that cause inflammation were discovered in the area of autistic children's brains responsible for memory and attention in a Tufts University study.

Gut Dysbiosis

Scientific studies support the hypothesis that gut microbiota affects neurodevelopment. One of them points out that an inflammation related to Gut Dysbiosis during development may likely cause autism.

Current Treatments

Current treatments are aimed only at reducing symptoms. In addition, drugs prescribed for children can have long-term side effects.

Many Research says;

Stem cells are an important option for autism treatment because of their ability to control the immune system and reduce inflammation.

Regenerative effect

Stem cells and exosomes can stimulate the regeneration and restoration of neurons in the damaged area by secreting messenger molecules and growth factors.

Improvements

-Better social communication abilities

-Reduced repetitive behaviors

-Decreased hyperactivity and aggression

-Decreased fear and anxiety
