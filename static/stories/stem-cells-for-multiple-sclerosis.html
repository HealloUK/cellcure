What is MS?

Multiple Sclerosis (MS) is an inflammatory autoimmune disease in which immune system cells attack the body's nerve cells that they are responsible for protecting.

Myelin Damage

As a result, the myelin sheath covering nerve cells is damaged and signals cannot be transmitted properly.

Symptoms

This situation causes many problems such as fatigue, numbness and tingling, vision problems, mobility problems etc.

Current Treatments

Current treatments aim to improve the patient's quality of life by reducing symptoms. But once damaged, they cannot repair nerve cells.

Scientific Evidences

Stem cells are a strong candidate for treating Multiple Sclerosis (MS) due to increasing evidence of their ability to reduce inflammation and modulate the immune system.

Restore & Delay

Studies have indicated that stem cells can help restore lost functioning and have the potential to delay or stop the progression of the disease.

What to expect?

With an increase in energy and muscle strength, a decrease in weariness, an improvement in mobility and balance, and many other factors, a general improvement in quality of life can be expected.
